CompactFlash

  In December of 1994, new company SunDisk(later rebranded as SanDisk to avoid confusion with Sun Microsystems) introduced to the market a new format of flash memory products that were small, used no power, and had no moving parts. CompactFlash is a portable storage device that is good for high capacity portable storage. It is a solid state device that uses flash memory, and consumes either 3 or 5 volts of power. Its main advantages are its portability, and the fact that itâ€™s less susceptible to data corruption than HDDs due to its lack of moving parts. When it was first introduced, its first 4 capacities were 2MB, 4MB, 10MB, and 15MB. Today, CompactFlash technology is still used by many DSLR(Digital Single-Lens Reflex) cameras.
